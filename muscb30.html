Week 2

Week 1: Learning Music

This was an extremely interesting module!! Since I have a bit of experience with music through choir, I felt I was able to connect a lot of my working knowledge with this new information. Overall, I think through choir I’ve learned to listen for melodies and basslines, but beats were something completely new. When we were asked to try to listen to the beat in Single Ladies, I listened to it so many times but still couldn’t find when the offbeat was. I think one thing I want to try is to to really listen for the beats in music and try to replicate them.

Additionally, basslines were something both familiar but also new. I was normally an alto or alto II (the lowest part in our women’s choir), so I sang a lot of the basslines. I recall singing the “ding dongs” in the Christmas song Carol of the Bells.

Despite my familiarity with singing basslines, I found trying to create something harder. Learning about how basslines can be an arpeggio helped me understand a little more, although at first what I made sounded more similar to melodies. I’ll try focusing on simplifying them and see if that helps. For now, similar to the beats, I’m going to try listening for the basslines in songs and replicating them either in future works or just by humming them.
